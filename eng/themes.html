Themes

Our innovation challenges are focused on high-level themes touching upon many different facets of the Department of National Defense and the Canadian Armed Forces. Themes are reviewed annually to reflect the continuously changing nature of operational and organizational needs.

COVID-19, pandemic response & public health

  • addressing moral injury in frontline workers
  • enabling rapid response for decision-making during pandemics with a focus on data gathering and analytics for community surveillance of contagious diseases
  • reuse of personal protective gear during a pandemic, including innovative material, design, and decontamination strategies
  • sanitization of equipment and workspaces in a pandemic setting, particularly to rapidly and effectively decontaminate commonly used equipment and surfaces

People and enhanced human performance

  • understanding, addressing, and managing post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) in defence and security positions, with a focus on diagnosis, prevention, training, education, resilience, and treatment
  • enhanced representation of women with the aim to recruit, retain, and 25% female representation by 2026. This includes non-traditional occupations where women are underrepresented
  • enhancing cognitive performance in Canadian Armed Forces (CAF) personnel, which includes augmented reality, advanced interactive visualization, and/or enhanced vision
  • predicting and optimizing personnel performance to achieve peak performance in individuals
  • physical and mental conditioning to enhance human performance in extreme climates/environments
  • improvement of ballistic protection so as to be more lightweight to improve mobility while maintaining optimal protection
  • collaboration of robotic systems to allow a single operator to have control over, and ability to coordinate, multiple robotic systems

Threat anticipation

  • detection/recognition/identification of objects/persons of interest
  • maritime surveillance and monitoring underwater threats
  • identification and characterization of space objects to improve space situational awareness for informed decision making for space system operations
  • solutions to aid in monitoring and interpreting full motion video (FMV) feeds, which includes detection/identification/tracking of events, people, and objects
  • making sense of social media data to extract useful and relevant information
  • cyber attribution for Canadian Defence and assessment of current cyberspace environment

Adapting to a changing environment

  • planning, detecting, early warning, and effective monitoring of chemical, biological, and radiological hazards
  • resilient non-GPS based positioning, navigation, and timing to equip military personnel with seamless 3D positioning and mapping
  • proactive deterrence to deter potential international crisis and war via anticipatory technologies
