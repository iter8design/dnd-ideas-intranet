Strategic focus areas

Our innovation challenges are focused on high-level strategic focus areas (SFA) touching upon many different facets of the Department of National Defense and the Canadian Armed Forces. SFA's are reviewed annually to reflect the continuously changing nature of operational and organizational needs.

People

People are central to everything we do to deliver on our mandate. DND/CAF are looking to:

  • Reflect and leverage Canadian diversity
  • Creatively attract and retain a tech-savvy generation amid a competitive milieu
  • Ensure our people are trained and prepared for future operating environments

Combat in virtual and physical environments

Conflicts will increasingly be waged above & below the threshold of armed conflict DND/CAF operators are looking to:

  • Use hybrid methods and influence operations in the grey zone
  • Achieve desired non-kinetic and kinetic effects
  • Withstand evolving hazards and threats such as cyber, deception, chemical and drones

Advanced platforms and weapons

The age of automation and novel weapons changes how low- and high-intensity conflicts are waged. CAF platforms and weapons are looking to:

  • Achieve the desired effects seamlessly across the land, sea, air, cyber and space domains
  • Build on advances in autonomy, sensing, materials, and supply chain integrity
  • Fight through attacks ranging from cyber to drone swarms

Accelerate command, control and intelligence

Consequential tactical decisions will need to be taken quickly amid deception and cyber-attacks. DND/CAF command and control capabilities are looking to:

  • Decentralize and semi-automate decisions, and adjust the application of force and lethality pan domains
  • Factor in cyber-attack, disruption, disinformation and deception
  • Leverage dual-use technology, such as intelligent advisors, data integrity and ubiquitous computing
  • Function in all relevant environments including the high Arctic

Defend North America

The return of near-peer state competitors outpaces North America’s defences. DND/CAF are looking to:

  • Modernize with the US the air and maritime warning and air defence capabilities of NORAD
  • Contribute to deter aggression in all domains
  • Leverage geography including the high Arctic and Canada’s approaches

Enable safety and security

As hazards evolve, safety and security demands grow more complex. National security, emergency management and first responders need to:

  • Employ decision support, all-domain awareness and improved threat detection
  • Benefit from robust physical equipment, information tools and resilient connectivity

The institution

Global trends compel the DND/CAF to renew itself. The institution is looking to:

  • Accelerate force development, procurement and capability updates
  • Become more agile in reacting to and meeting the challenges of the future operating environment
  • Embrace enterprise data analytics to inform management and procurement decisions

Evolution of science and technology

Globalization of S&T could diminish the dominance of the US and allies in warfare.

  • Anticipate security changes sparked by the spread of S&T advances
  • Harness S&T to modernize its institution, practices and capabilities
  • Interoperate with allies’ technologically-advanced forces
  • Disrupt and surprise adversaries
