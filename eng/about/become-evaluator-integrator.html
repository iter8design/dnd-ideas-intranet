Become a technology evaluator/integrator

Technology evaluators/integrators are key to the success of the program. Your defence and security experience combined with sound scientific expertise uniquely position you to define the impact an innovation may have on defence capability. The evaluator/integrator role grows in relevance as an innovation moves from its conceptual origin.

You are DND/CAF subject-matter experts who are assigned to monitor an IDEaS project’s progress, act as a liaison between the innovator and DND, and assess the viability of an IDEaS project as a solution to one of the IDEaS program’s defence and security challenges.

Technology evaluators/integrators leverage their scientific, defence, and security knowledge and expertise to provide key insights and identify linkages to DND, with the goal of advancing IDEaS projects toward “adoption ready” solutions.

Roles and responsibilities

  • To be the science and technology interface for innovators to help increase their understanding of the defence and security domain
  • To provide a scientific assessment on each contractual submission
  • To provide a global insight on the science and technology advances achieved and how they might be implemented into other programming or developmental efforts

For contractual reasons, evaluators/integrators must not influence the direction or modify the scope of an innovators project. They should seek opportunities for defence capability to be augmented.vFor leveraging and coherence considerations within IDEaS, a dedicated group of military officers and defence scientists will formulate and coordinate the science and technology outputs of all challenges to the Level 1's.

Time commitments

Your time commitment will vary based on the stage in which the challenge is in. For competitive projects, projects in stage 1A are 5 weeks a year, stage 1B are 2 weeks per year, and negotiated as required for stage 2.

Projects using other program elements are negotiated based on the needs of each challenge.

Professional development

Being a technology evaluator/integrator can help you:

  • increase your exposure to outside innovation and opportunity, thus increasing your opportunity to adapt, integrate and use existing science and technology
  • grow your network and creating effective working relationships within and outside of DRDC
  • receive recognition for your work through representation of DRDC in your advisory, coordination, and liaison roles
  • demonstrate scientific leadership through the scientific judgement and evaluation of IDEaS proposals and deliverables
  • gain exposure to senior decision-makers while providing authoritative advice
